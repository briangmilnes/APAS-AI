Chap47clean Hash Table Benchmarks
==================================

All benchmarks configured with:
- Warmup: 300ms
- Measurement: 1000ms  
- Total per benchmark: ~1.3s
- Sizes tested: 500 and 1000 elements

Results Summary (per operation):
---------------------------------

Linear Probing:
  Insert/500:   ~33 µs  (660 ops/iteration)
  Insert/1000:  ~68 µs  (1320 ops/iteration)
  Lookup/500:   ~9.5 µs
  Lookup/1000:  ~24 µs

Quadratic Probing:
  Insert/500:   ~35 µs
  Insert/1000:  ~84 µs
  Lookup/500:   ~9.7 µs
  Lookup/1000:  ~21 µs

Double Hashing:
  Insert/500:   ~31 µs  (fastest insert!)
  Insert/1000:  ~86 µs
  Lookup/500:   ~10 µs
  Lookup/1000:  ~20 µs

Vec Chaining:
  Insert/500:   ~68 µs  (slower due to chains)
  Insert/1000:  ~249 µs
  Lookup/500:   ~57 µs  (slower - searches chains)
  Lookup/1000:  ~174 µs

Performance Insights:
---------------------
1. Flat tables (open addressing) are 2-3x faster than chaining for these workloads
2. Double hashing has best insert performance at smaller sizes
3. All three probing strategies have similar lookup times (~20-24µs for 1000 items)
4. Vec chaining shows O(n) behavior in chains (load factor ~10:1)

APAS Compliance:
----------------
- All timings respect 1.3s constraint per benchmark
- Measurements use ~30 samples for statistical validity
- Each benchmark tests 2 different sizes to show scalability
